---
title: Epistemic Protocol Layer
description: "Rules and workflows that harden knowledge systems against collapse"
---

An epistemic protocol layer is a set of rules, checks, and workflows that harden
a knowledge system against error cascades. It functions like a constitution: it
specifies how claims enter, how they are challenged, and what traceability is
required before they gain authority.

In practice, it includes sovereignty, traceability, and default adversarial
validation. The goal is not to slow thinking, but to keep correction cheaper
than drift, especially under the pressure of
[Encyclopedia Meltdown](./encyclopedia-meltdown).
