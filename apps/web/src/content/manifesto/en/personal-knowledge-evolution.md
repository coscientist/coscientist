Personal knowledge evolution traces the development of tools and ideas for
managing personal knowledge, from mid-20th century visions to contemporary
systems.

## Foundational Vision

- [As We May Think](./as-we-may-think) — [Vannevar Bush](./vannevar-bush)'s 1945
  essay
- [Memex](./memex) — Bush's proposed personal knowledge machine

## Conceptual Frameworks

- [Digital Brain](./digital-brain) — a system that mirrors and extends a mind
- [Digital Garden](./digital-garden) — public, evolving interlinked notes
- [Digital Jungle](./digital-jungle) — self-organizing low-friction knowledge
  space
- [Second Brain](./second-brain) — personal knowledge management practice

## Implementations

- [Project Aldehyde](./project-aldehyde) — [Sunghyun Cho](./sunghyun-cho)'s
  early attempt
- [Extracranial](./extracranial) — Cho's personal digital brain system
- [Multilingual Memex](./multilingual-memex) — language-aware knowledge system
  with auto-translation
- [Project PIRI](./project-piri) — cancelled translation initiative

## Design Principle

- [Friction as Enemy](./friction-as-enemy) — minimizing overhead to maximize
  capture
